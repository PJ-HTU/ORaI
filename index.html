The Challenge

Why real-world OR problems remain unsolved

Commercial solvers and textbook algorithms were not designed for the messy, large-scale realities of industrial operations. Three fundamental barriers stand in the way.

Commercial solvers hit a wall at industrial scale

Tools like Gurobi and CPLEX work well on textbook instances. But real industrial problems — involving thousands of jobs, vehicles, or assets with complex constraints — exceed what any off-the-shelf solver can handle within practical time limits.

Custom algorithms take months of expert effort

When solvers fall short, the only option is a hand-crafted heuristic. Designing one demands deep OR expertise, careful problem analysis, and months of iterative engineering — a process most organizations simply cannot afford to repeat for every new challenge.

Algorithms don't transfer across problems

A metaheuristic tuned for one scheduling problem rarely generalizes to another — even structurally similar ones. Every new problem variant restarts the design process from scratch, with no reuse of prior investment in algorithm development.

Our Approach

ORaI: LLM-driven automated algorithm design

Most AI methods try to solve optimization problems directly — and fail on anything novel or large-scale. ORaI takes a different path: we use large language models to design the algorithms themselves, producing a portfolio of complementary heuristics that collectively cover your problem's full landscape of instances and runtime requirements.

Stage 01

Modular Problem Decomposition

We encode your problem as modular components — state representation, action space, and a domain tool library. This separation means our LLM focuses purely on decision logic, not on re-learning your domain from scratch. It also enables generalization: the same structure works across radically different problem types.

Stage 02

Complementary Portfolio Evolution

An LLM-driven evolutionary process iteratively generates, evaluates, and selects heuristics under explicit runtime budgets. Rather than producing a single algorithm, it constructs a complementary portfolio — heuristics that collectively dominate across instance types, from fast emergency decisions to deep overnight optimization.

Delivery

Production-Ready Algorithm Portfolio

You receive clean, executable Python code — a set of algorithms that outperform both human-designed heuristics and existing LLM-based methods, and that generalize zero-shot even to problems no prior method has encountered. No problem-specific pretraining required.
